Licensing Guide

A step-by-step resource for legal firearms ownership in India under the Arms Rules 2016.

The Application Process

Owning a firearm in India is a privilege governed by the Ministry of Home Affairs. The process ensures that only responsible, law-abiding citizens are granted licenses.

1

Eligibility Check

Ensure you meet the basic criteria: Minimum age of 21, no criminal record, and a genuine need for self-protection, crop protection, or sports shooting.

2

Submit Form III

Fill out the application form (Form III) and submit it to your local Licensing Authority (usually the Deputy Commissioner or Police Commissioner).

Download Form III (PDF)
3

Police Verification

Your local police station will conduct a background check and verify your residence and character. This is a critical stage of the process.

4

Interview & Approval

In many cases, an interview with the Licensing Authority is required. If approved, your license will be issued with a Unique Identification Number (UIN).

What is a UIN?

The Unique Identification Number (UIN) is a lifetime identification number issued to every licensee. It is mandatory for all transactions, including purchase, repair, and ammunition acquisition. Without a UIN, a license is considered incomplete under the NDAL (National Database of Arms Licenses).

Required Documents

  • Identity Proof (Aadhar, Passport, or Voter ID)
  • Address Proof (Utility bills or Rent agreement)
  • Age Proof (Birth certificate or School leaving certificate)
  • Medical Certificate (Form S-3)
  • Passport size photographs
  • Income Tax Returns (last 3 years)
